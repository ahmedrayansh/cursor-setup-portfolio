## Key Points
- Great B2B content needs a distribution system or it will disappear.
- One asset should be atomized into multiple LinkedIn posts, videos, community shares, emails, and other formats.
- Master one channel first, then expand distribution across the places buyers actually research and talk.

## Transcript Notes

### Content Must Be Marketed
The session's premise is that creating content is not enough. B2B marketers often publish a strong piece once, share it once, and then move on. Ross argues that the second half of the job is marketing the content repeatedly and strategically.

### Atomize and Amplify
After a major asset goes live, break it into smaller useful parts. A podcast question can become a short video. A key insight can become a LinkedIn post. A section can become a checklist or email. The goal is to turn one asset into many buyer touchpoints.

### LinkedIn First, Then Diversify
Ross recommends mastering one channel before trying to distribute everywhere. For many B2B SaaS teams, LinkedIn is the first strong channel. Once there is content-market fit on LinkedIn, teams can adapt winning ideas to Reddit, YouTube, newsletters, communities, and AI-search-visible sources.

### Distribution and the Modern Buyer Journey
Buyers research across many surfaces, including LinkedIn, Reddit, Google, YouTube, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Slack groups, and review sites. A useful B2B SaaS content strategy should make the brand present in the places where buyers form opinions, not only on the company blog.
