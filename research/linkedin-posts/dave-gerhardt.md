## Post 1

Date: 2026-03-20  
Topic: Exit Five as a B2B media company and community  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_four-years-into-building-exit-five-weve-activity-7440798710302674944-bL_s

Summary: Dave describes Exit Five's evolution from a solo project into a six-person media and community business with thousands of members, a newsletter, a podcast, and 12M+ expected content reach in 2026. He connects the growth of the brand with in-person community moments, private conversations, and a strong sense of belonging among B2B marketing leaders.

Research takeaway: A B2B SaaS brand can use LinkedIn organic as part of a larger media-company motion. The strongest posts are not isolated updates; they point back to a repeatable ecosystem of community, events, podcasting, newsletter, and founder voice.

## Post 2

Date: 2026-03-12  
Topic: CMO priorities, AI adoption, and proving marketing impact  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_we-polled-100-cmos-at-companies-50m-500m-activity-7437864589309927426-B56t

Summary: Dave shares themes from a poll of roughly 100 CMOs at $50M-$500M+ ARR companies. The strongest themes are practical AI adoption, pressure to grow with less spend, and a need to connect marketing to revenue outcomes beyond MQL volume.

Research takeaway: LinkedIn posts built from proprietary audience research are high-value content. They give the author authority, give peers useful benchmarks, and create a reason for the right audience to engage.

## Post 3

Date: 2026-02-26  
Topic: AI turning hard-won copywriting judgment into reusable systems  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_took-me-15-years-to-be-a-decent-copywriter-activity-7432747547439517696-6tyW

Summary: Dave reflects on spending 15 years learning copywriting only to see that knowledge become easier to package into AI-assisted workflows such as a Claude Skill. The tone is self-aware, but the useful point is serious: durable marketing judgment still matters, yet it can now be operationalized and distributed through tools.

Research takeaway: Good founder content can mix a timely AI observation with lived experience. For SaaS, this suggests a content angle around turning internal expertise into reusable prompts, workflows, templates, and enablement assets.

### LinkedIn as the Primary PR Channel

Date: 2025-03-03  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_if-i-were-running-marketing-at-a-b2b-startup-activity-7302317860821913600-M7pi

Summary: Dave argues that a B2B startup should treat LinkedIn as a core PR channel. Founders and leaders across marketing, sales, and customer success should post several times per week about customer calls, pain points, lessons from building, and the buyer's day-to-day life.

### Becoming the Best Resource for the Buyer

Date: 2024-06-29  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_this-is-my-favorite-b2b-marketing-strategy-activity-7212771794917744640-AG3R

Summary: Dave's core B2B content strategy is to become the best resource in a specific niche through LinkedIn, articles, video, community, and events. He emphasizes a clear point of view and making customers or early adopters part of the story.

Research takeaway: Organic content should not only promote the product. It should make the company feel like the best place to learn about the buyer's problem.
