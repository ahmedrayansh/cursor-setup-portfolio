## Post 1

Date: 2026-04-03  
Topic: Distribution as the missing content problem  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_every-marketer-has-had-this-moment-you-activity-7445892892096745472-alZk

Summary: Ross says many marketers do not have a content quality problem. They publish a strong post, share it once on LinkedIn, move on, and then wonder why it produced no pipeline impact. The real issue is distribution.

Research takeaway: A LinkedIn strategy should include redistribution and repurposing from the beginning. Publishing once is not a strategy.

## Post 2

Date: 2026-02-20  
Topic: Non-linear B2B buyer journeys  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-activity-7430609231441031168-hY-N

Summary: Ross argues that buyers research across LinkedIn, Reddit, Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, review sites, Slack channels, and private chats. He pushes against a narrow view of content that only includes blogs and landing pages.

Research takeaway: B2B SaaS content should be distributed where buyers form opinions, not only where analytics tools are easiest to measure.

## Post 3

Date: 2026-02-17  
Topic: Repurposing and reposting high-performing assets  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_b2b-content-distribution-masterclass-with-activity-7429623577907417088-rS74

Summary: Ross promotes a content distribution masterclass and states that content marketing has two parts: creating the content and marketing it. He recommends repurposing every high-performing asset, reposting because most people missed it the first time, and being strategic with timing, channels, and formats.

Research takeaway: The same idea should be translated into multiple formats and channels. The goal is not repetition for its own sake; it is extending the useful life of the strongest idea.
