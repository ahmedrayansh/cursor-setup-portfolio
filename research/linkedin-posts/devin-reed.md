## Post 1

Date: 2025-12-19  
Topic: Launching a YouTube strategy by repurposing the content vault  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_i-finally-made-the-leap-and-launched-my-youtube-activity-7407858714407448578-8nUs

Summary: Devin explains that his first YouTube phase was not about producing a brand-new show from scratch. He repurposed existing videos from podcasts, keynotes, and collaborations, growing Shorts views from roughly 1,000 to 37,800 in the first 30 days.

## Post 2

Date: 2025-12-17  
Topic: The marketing funnel does not describe buyer behavior  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_i-once-presented-a-content-plan-to-the-c-suite-activity-7407108176371916800-oFZ4

Summary: Devin frames the classic marketing funnel as too linear for modern B2B buying. Buyers discover, compare, trust, and remember brands across multiple touchpoints long before they fill out a form or take a tracked conversion action.

Research takeaway: LinkedIn organic should create trust before the buyer is actively in-market. The content plan should serve the 95% who are not ready to buy yet, not only the 5% who are.

## Post 3

Date: 2025-10-15  
Topic: CEO LinkedIn playbook that drove 8.9M views  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/devinreed_one-of-my-ceo-clients-started-posting-on-activity-7384276841542500352-x4uT

Summary: Devin shares a five-step executive LinkedIn playbook: pick one word to own, identify 2-3 content pillars, commit to output, set clear goals, and keep measuring growth across the year. The example generated 8.9M views in 12 months for a CEO client.

Research takeaway: Executive LinkedIn strategy needs a narrow mental anchor and repeatable pillars. The goal is not "post more"; it is to make the executive impossible to ignore for a specific topic.
