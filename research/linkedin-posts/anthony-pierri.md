## Post 1

Date: 2026-02-23  
Topic: Firmographics are not a marketable segment  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anthonypierri_weve-worked-on-500-positioning-projects-activity-7431832819489955840-Nr4H

Summary: Anthony argues that describing an ICP with firmographics like industry, region, employee count, or stage does not prove the market has a shared need. He recommends segmenting around either an existing product category in a mature market or a job-to-be-done in an immature market.

Research takeaway: B2B SaaS LinkedIn content can win by teaching founders to replace vague ICP language with sharper buying-context language.

## Post 2

Date: 2025-12-19  
Topic: Existing category vs. creating new mental space  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anthonypierri_the-two-most-important-positioning-strategies-activity-7407779981520564224-E0xl

Summary: Anthony explains that positioning choices often start with a core decision: fight for a spot in an existing category or attach the product to a task, workflow, or initiative where there is open mental space.

Research takeaway: Good product marketing content gives founders a decision tree, not just definitions. LinkedIn posts that clarify tradeoffs are useful for early-stage SaaS audiences.
