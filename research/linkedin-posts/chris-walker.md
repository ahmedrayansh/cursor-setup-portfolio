## Post 2

Date: 2024-07-11  
Topic: Cutting low-ROI Google Ads spend  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_gotomarket-b2b-marketing-activity-7217166322420318209-ki_j

Summary: Chris describes recommending an 88% Google Ads budget cut for an $80M ARR SaaS company after finding poor ROI on non-branded and competitor search spend. He frames the decision around CAC payback, pipeline decline, win-rate decline, and reallocating budget to better-performing programs.

Research takeaway: Strong LinkedIn content often works when it presents a specific business diagnosis with numbers. For B2B SaaS, posts that challenge default budget allocations can create valuable debate and attract senior buyers.

## Post 3

Date: 2024-06-19  
Topic: Trade show spend and changed B2B discovery behavior  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_gotomarket-marketing-events-activity-7209193355774160897-ritY

Summary: Chris argues that large national and regional trade show booth spending often performs at low ROI because B2B product discovery has moved beyond conferences. Buyers now discover and validate products through peers, social, content platforms, online communities, and digital research.

Research takeaway: LinkedIn organic can be positioned as part of the modern discovery layer that replaced older event-heavy discovery. The post is useful for arguing that content and distribution should get budget, not just events.

### Low-Engagement Posts Can Drive Revenue

Date: 2023-01-06  
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_b2b-marketing-content-activity-7017125539991740416-X69p

Summary: Chris argues that some of his lowest-engagement posts drive the most revenue because they challenge accepted thinking and spark high-intent conversations, even if they do not maximize likes.

Research takeaway: B2B SaaS teams should not judge LinkedIn only by engagement. Posts that create belief change can matter more than broadly agreeable content.
